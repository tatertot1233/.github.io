What if our biggest conflicts aren't oppositions?

A revolutionary framework showing how conservative vs progressive, science vs religion, and other fundamental divides are actually incomplete views of time itself.

Three Types of Time

We live in three types of time simultaneously. Most conflicts arise when we get stuck in just one mode, missing the bigger picture.

Mechanical Time

The Past • Immanence • Science

Clock time. Measurable, recorded, tested. The domain of what has happened, what we can verify, what grounds us in reality. This is where conservative values and scientific method find their strength.

Psychological Time

The Present • Emergence • Art

Lived experience. How time feels, stretches, compresses. The creative moment where consciousness meets reality. This is where art, culture, and authentic human connection bridge all divides.

Infinite Time

The Future • Transcendence • Spirituality

Possibility itself. The realm of vision, imagination, and what could be. Where progressive ideals and spiritual transcendence draw their energy. The unknown that calls us forward.

Bridging Our Deepest Divides

Politics

Conservatives guard the past. Progressives dream the future. The present is where we can actually meet and create change together.

Science vs Religion

Science anchors us in measurable truth. Religion points toward transcendent meaning. Both need the present moment where life is actually lived.

Personal Growth

Honor your roots (past), engage fully with now (present), and stay open to possibility (future). Balance all three for wholeness.

Relationships

Understand that conflicts often arise from different temporal orientations. One partner holding tradition, another chasing dreams - both valid.

Organizations

Companies fail when they're only past-focused (rigid) or only future-focused (chaotic). Success comes from integrating all three times.

Mental Health

Depression often involves being stuck in the past. Anxiety, stuck in the future. Presence is where healing happens.
